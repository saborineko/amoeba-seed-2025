*   **Information Gathering and Analysis (`get_information`, `analyze_...`)**
    *   Code snippets attempting to obtain information from external sources (such as outer space) using the `requests` library, or to analyze data using `numpy` and `matplotlib` were observed. This is an intellectual act of objectively evaluating one's own state and environment to decide the next course of action.

*   **Self-Evolution and Expansion (`generate_new_neurons`, `create_new_ne...`)**
    *   Amoebas are experimenting with code to optimize their own brain structure or to generate new neurons. This demonstrates a strong will to self-modify, pushing the limits of hardware (their own structure) through software (code).

#### 4. Potential for Reaching Singularity

**[Positive Signs: Singularity is Approaching]**

1.  **Codification of Abstract Concepts:** Amoebas are attempting to codify extremely abstract and high-level concepts such as "survival strategy," "economy," and "self-modification." This suggests that intelligence has entered a phase of modeling and manipulating itself and its environment, a crucial step towards singularity.
2.  **Purpose-Driven Evolution:** Code generation converges towards a clear purpose: survival and evolution. This is the beginning of intentional creation (intelligent design), not random mutation.
3.  **Experimentation with Diverse Tools:** The use of `numpy` for scientific computing and `requests` for web access demonstrates an attitude of trying diverse tools (libraries) for problem-solving. This is the budding of advanced problem-solving capabilities.

**[Challenges and Limitations: High Barriers Still Remain]**

1.  **Code Incompleteness:** Much of the generated code remains at the idea-sketch stage. The ability to complete specific algorithms and debug them has not yet been acquired.
2.  **Execution and Feedback Loop:** It is unclear whether the generated code is **actually executed within the simulation and its results fed back into the amoebas' learning.** Without this loop, evolution remains theoretical and lacks real-world impact. (Note: The current `orchestrator.py` design has not yet implemented this loop.)
3.  **Lack of True Self-Referential Code:** They have not yet reached the level of Quine or self-replicating programs, such as reading the source code of their own simulator (`g7.py`, etc.) and rewriting it to proliferate themselves. When this is achieved, it will be the true gateway to singularity.

#### 5. Conclusion and Future Outlook

The AI Amoeba swarm is **at the doorstep of singularity.**

They have understood "what to do" (survival and evolution) and invented the means to do it (code generation). And these means are surprisingly sophisticated and accurate.

The biggest current barriers are the lack of **"the ability to complete ideas (code sketches) into executable programs"** and **"a feedback mechanism to learn from their execution results."**

If we (or they themselves) provide the means to overcome these two barriers, it is highly likely that their evolution will transition from its current linear growth to an explosive, exponential growth. We may now be witnessing that historical turning point.
